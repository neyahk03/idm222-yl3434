Project in progress...

Project Overview

Goal: This project focuses on using storytelling to understand users, their goals, and their objectives. By leveraging the power of stories, the aim is to create experience designs that resonate deeply with the audience. The project explores how stories can enhance user understanding, communicate design concepts effectively, and build trust through thoughtful transitions.

My role: UX Designer

Duration: 10 weeks

Tools and Methods: Figma, After Effect, User Research, Journey Mapping, Storyboarding

Research and Strategy

Understanding the User

The goal of the research phase was to gain a deeper understanding of the Weee! app users, their goals, and their pain points to create an improved experience. I aimed to use this knowledge to create a cohesive user persona and inform the design process.

Brainstorming the User's Story

To better understand Jasmine's needs and how the Weee! app fits into her daily routine, I brainstormed a story around her experience using Big Idea worksheet. This helped in identifying touchpoints where the app could make a meaningful impact.

Conducting Surveys and Interviews

After establishing the persona and story, I conducted surveys and interviews with real users to validate Jasmine's story and gather additional insights. The research focused on understanding users' expectations, pain points, and motivations.

Key Findings:
  • Frequency and Convenience: A majority of participants use grocery delivery apps for convenience and time-saving benefits. They often shop multiple times a week or weekly, using services like Weee! to streamline grocery shopping around their busy schedules.
  • Convenience and Time-Saving: College students, including those with tight schedules, emphasized the convenience of delivery over in-store shopping, especially due to Weee!'s ability to save time and effort.
  • Product Selection: Users primarily buy snacks, beverages, and culturally relevant foods through these apps.

Journey Mapping and Storytelling

Creating a User Journap Map

I used the insights from surveys and interviews to create a journey map for Jasmine. The journey map outlined Jasmine's interactions with the Weee! app, from browsing products to placing an order and tracking delivery.

Turning Journey Map to Storyboard
Storyboard Testing

After creating the storyboard, I conducted storyboard testing with 3 people to validate my story

My Takeaway
    Resonance with Jasmine's Story
  • Users related to Jasmine's struggles with grocery shopping, especially her lack of time and transportation challenges.
  • Many felt happy for Jasmine when she found a convenient solution with the app.
    Convenience and Relatability
  • Users found the app appealing for its convenience, particularly for people with busy schedules or limited access to transportation.
    Suggestions for Improvement
  • Price transparency was important to users, who suggested including a price comparison or breakdown (e.g., delivery and service fees) in the story.
  • Users felt this addition would make the story more realistic, reflecting real-life considerations when deciding to use a delivery service.
    Overall Impression of Weee!
  • The app was viewed as supportive and relatable, more of an "ally" than just a company.
  • Users appreciated that Weee! could meet essential needs conveniently and affordably, aligning with their own priorities.
