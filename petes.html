ordering app for pete's little lunch box food truck

Project Overview

Description:

This project is part of the User Experience course taught by Professor Chris Nappi. It involved designing a take-out app for Pete's Little Lunch Box, a popular food truck near Drexel University. Known for its quick, customizable meals but lacking branding, Pete's was an ideal candidate for a digital makeover. Through collaborative efforts, our team utilized user research, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing to create an app that reflected Pete's charm and practicality while addressing user needs and aligning with its values.

My Role: UX Designer

My team: Yi Huan Yang, Sreeja Satish, and Emma Houghton

Timeline: September 2024 - December 2024

Tools and Technologies: Figma, User Research, Affinity Mapping, NUF Framework, User Journey Map

Challenge

Who is Pete's?

Pete's is an American style breakfast and lunch food truck located on Drexel University's campus. They provide hearty and efficient meals at a low cost for students and workers in the area. Pete's meals fill up both stomachs and hearts.

We chose Pete's Little Lunch Box for its strong community presence and charm, aiming to translate this into a mobile app. Despite a small menu, their customizable options created a deceptively long list, challenging us to simplify it without overwhelming users. Additionally, balancing our team's workload and schedules proved difficult, but through communication and efficient use of class time, we collaborated effectively to deliver a comprehensive app.

Our Goal

Through our project, our main goal was to:

Design an efficient and intuitive app

Reflect Pete's consistent reliability, friendly service, and commitment to quality

Cater to the fast-paced needs of on-the-go consumers
